Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

"People's President" and "Missile Man of India"

Biography

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, was a multifaceted personality. His journey began in a humble household, where he imbibed values of simplicity and integrity. Despite facing financial constraints, he pursued education with unwavering determination.

After completing his aerospace engineering degree from the Madras Institute of Technology, Dr. Kalam joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO). His early career saw significant contributions to India's space and missile programs, culminating in the successful launch of the satellite "Rohini" in 1980.

Dr. Kalam's exceptional leadership qualities and technical acumen led to his appointment as the Chief Scientific Advisor to the Prime Minister. Subsequently, he played a pivotal role in India's nuclear tests, showcasing the nation's prowess in defense technology.

Contributions

Often hailed as the "Missile Man of India," Dr. Kalam's visionary leadership spearheaded the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP). This initiative resulted in the successful development of Agni and Prithvi missiles, bolstering India's defense capabilities.

Dr. Kalam's tenure as the 11th President of India (2002-2007) was marked by his commitment to education, science, and technology. He envisioned an empowered and knowledgeable society and continued to engage with students, delivering inspirational lectures across the country.

Post his presidency, Dr. Kalam dedicated himself to teaching, authoring books, and encouraging innovation among the youth. His indomitable spirit and positive outlook left an indelible mark on the hearts of millions, earning him the title of the "People's President."
